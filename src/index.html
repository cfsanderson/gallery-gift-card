Justin Linn

Going through school I was constantly frustrated trying to find a career that both I was good at and enjoyed. I found myself always caught in between creative and analytical skills that didn’t quite have a home in one industry. Due to this feeling of feeling like the jack of all trades but not quite ‘belonging’ to one field of study I will never forget the first time I got my hands dirty writing some code. The idea of creating, and improving technology that enhanced my life on a daily basis was so thrilling, but also overwhelming.

I could have not imagined a better place to grow my new skills. The Iron Yard - St. Petersburg was by far one of the most rigorous, and supportive environments I have ever experienced. I can honestly say that I would not be the same person without technology, nor would I be the same developer without The Iron Yard. Going into The Iron Yard I could put together a simple website using HTML and CSS. Three months later I can make site mockups, fully responsive websites, make an API using ExpressJS, and build a dynamic front end in React. I have never felt so empowered in just three months. It is a real change of perspective to think that I can make anything I can imagine with time being the only boundary to my success.

There is absolutely no substitute for this program, community, or The Iron Yard staff. I know The Iron Yard closing has been hard on all the staff and student, but I think in the long run it will be all for the best. I am excited to see what the future has to offer for our community, and all the new opportunities for growth. This program changed my life forever, and I would have not learned as much as I have without The Iron Yard.
